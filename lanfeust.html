Lanfeust of Troy is a long-running French language comic series written by Christophe Arleston, drawn by Didier Tarquin, and published by Soleil Productions.

Synopsis

On the world of Troy, most humans have a single magic power. Each power is unique to the person and includes such diverse feats as walking on water, personal teleportation, and causing thirst in others. However, none can use their power unless in the close or remote presence of a sage of Eckmül, Troy's version of a studied magician.

Lanfeust is an orphan and an apprentice blacksmith who can heat metal at will. His adventure begins when he is asked to mend the sword of a passing aristocrat. When handling the sword's pommel, which was made of ivory from the Magohamoth—a legendary magical beast—Lanfeust gains the power to use any power known in Troy, more so without the presence of a sage. Nicolede, both the local sage and Lanfeust's future father-in-law, persuades Lanfeust to accompany him and his daughters C'ian and Cixi to the University of Magic in Eckmül in order to learn more about this phenomenon.

Among the many perils faced during their journey, they are ultimately confronted by Thanos, a pirate with the same potential as Lanfeust who will do whatever it takes to steal the ivory of the Magohamoth.

Publication

Ivory of the Magohamoth (FR: L'Ivoire du Magohamoth) (1994)

Troy is a fascinating world, where magic is part of everyone's daily life. Everyone has a power, small or large, more or less useful. One has the gift of congealing water into ice, the other the power to emit colored farts... Lanfeust knows how to melt metal with a single glance. In his village, he naturally became an apprentice with the blacksmith. But the touch of a sword coming from the distant Baronnies reveals in him an exceptional power; he no longer has a unique gift but absolute and unlimited power.

Accompanied by the wise old Nicolède and his two daughters with such dissimilar characters, Lanfeust is carried away in a whirlwind of adventures during which he meets the most incongruous, the most surprising and the most dangerous of creatures.

Thanos the Discordant (FR: Thanos l'incongru) (June 1995)

The characteristic of the world of Troy is to give each being a magical power, whatever it is, big or small, sometimes somewhat incongruous. In an operation close to role-playing, the characters move according to this power and advance the action with a very successful sense of psychology. Lanfeust the virgin is flanked by two luscious creatures. One heals the wounds at nightfall, she is his betrothed; the other transforms into ice whatever she wants, if she wants. She especially chills Lanfeust with her fiery temper. Between the two, the ingenuous balances and the end of the cycle will see him reach the supreme quest, if not ultimate: the discovery of love. In the meantime, the reader will have discovered Hébus, a loud-mouthed and very short Troll who transforms an army into drumsticks of poultry, punctuating with Brussels oaths a carnage perpetrated with carnivorous joy; Thanos, Lanfeust's evil double, himself capable of accessing absolute power and capable, because such is his power, of going from one point to another in the known universe at will; the brave knight Gold-Azur whose good blood does not lie; Master Nicolède finally, a good-natured sage, incidentally father of the two suitors of the title hero...

Castle Or-Azur (FR: Castel Or-Azur) (April 1996)

Always in search of the sword of the knight Or-Azur, Lanfeust and his companions, after many adventures, finally reach the castle. But the knight and the magic sword are gone. However, they find a clue that will lead them directly to Magohamoth. The continuation of their search then leads them to the Culhaig tournament, at the other end of the Baronnies...

The Paladin from Eckmül (FR: Le Paladin d'Eckmül) (November 1996)

The Tremor of the Haruspex (FR: Le Frisson de l'Haruspice) (October 1997)

Empress Cixi (FR: Cixi Impératrice) (October 1998)

In the world of Troy reign the gods, the sages of Eckmül who relay from one village to another the magic specific to each individual, trolls and other monsters. While separated from Cixi, Master Nicolède, his second daughter C'ian, Lanfeust, Hébus and the god Sphax travel by train to find the master of the gods, the Magohamoth. Along the way, they narrowly escape a bloody attack perpetrated by white trolls. Meanwhile, Cixi turns around and joins Thanos Averroës, Lanfeust's sworn enemy, who can teleport, who is also very powerful and who has taken it into his head to rid the country of all its sages. He reigns terror. Having reached the City of the Gods, our heroes discover the extravagance of divine daily life and negotiate a guide to progress in their quest. Go to the City of Ice, where new adventures await them.

Petaures Hide in Order to Die (FR: Les Pétaures Se Cachent pour Mourir) (October 1999)

What a journey since that calm day when Lanfeust's powerful powers were revealed to him. At the end of the previous episode, he was in a very bad position. Hébus the troll dives to his rescue in the icy waters of the Far North. Will they ever find the City of Ice where a guide was to appear to lead them to their ultimate goal, the mythical Magohamoth? Meanwhile in the city of Eckmül, the invincible Thanos reinforces his dominance. All the sages are neutralized and the inhabitants, deprived of magic. But the resistance is organized, led by the intrepid Dark Shadow. In this penultimate episode of the series, winks and jokes follow one another to the swirling rhythm of the adventures of this friendly team.

The Fabulous Beast (FR: La Bête Fabuleuse) (December 2000)

The Fabulous Beast (FR: La Bête Fabuleuse) (December 2000)

Nothing is going right in the world of Troy. The sages, relays of the magic, disappear one by one. Lanfeust himself lost his power. Who is this megalomaniac who plunges towns and villages into chaos? And what about this gigantic tree from which all the evils that overwhelm them seem to come? Lanfeust and Hébus, reunited again, go to Delpont to investigate these strange disappearances...
